Lights, Camera, Eco-Action: Inside Netflix's Green Documentary Collection

By Krystal Won

For the past few years, Netflix has been my primary source of entertainment during leisurely afternoons and evenings. Among the vast array of offerings, I found myself particularly drawn to nature & science documentaries, which comprise a significant portion of my viewing list. As an enthusiast of the environment and its many mysteries, I was inspired to transform this interest into a project. Using an existing dataset from Kaggle and Python for data analysis, I embarked on a journey to assess the extent of Netflix's environmentally-focused content up until September 2021.

Recognizing the complexities of Netflix's operations, I understood that the availability of some titles fluctuates due to licensing agreements . This presented a challenge to obtaining a comprehensive list of current and past Netflix titles through web scraping.

Nevertheless, I resolved to analyze the shows that were once, or still are, available on the platform. My focus was directed towards the production countries, aiming to discover which nations invest the most in environmental documentaries.

US and UK Lead in the Production of Environmentally-Themed Documentaries

The first stage of my journey involved tallying the number of eco-documentaries available for each country.

At the front of the pack, the United States and the United Kingdom demonstrate a robust output of environmental documentaries, with 26 and 24 titles respectively. It is also notable that the US and UK co-produced several prominent titles such as "Nature's Great Events: Diaries", "Trophy", and "Our Planet", which cover a wide range of topics -- from mammal preservation to climate change -- under the umbrella of environmental discourse.

Yet, the sheer volume of their documentary production dilutes the eco-focus when we take a broader view.

Specifically, environmental films account for about 5% of the U.S.'s total documentary content, a figure dwarfed by the United Kingdom's more impressive 18.9%. According to the analysis, this substantial percentage signifies the UK's pronounced commitment to environmental discourse, despite a smaller overall output compared to its American counterpart.

Another intriguing result is that, the country that emerges with the most pronounced environmental lean in its documentary production is Brazil. With a third of its 12 documentaries orbiting around environmental topics, Brazil's output ratio is a testament to the country's acute awareness of its ecological narrative.

The Course of Environmental Awareness in the UK through Netflix's Documentaries

One region that particularly piqued my interest was the United Kingdom. Intrigued by the rich history and diversity of its documentary landscape, I decided to delve deeper.

I examined the release years of each environmental documentary, hoping to trace the ebb and flow of eco-awareness over time.

This deep-dive into the UK's green content unveiled an intriguing timeline which helped demonstrating how the nation's environmental awareness has evolved over the years.

Notably, the production of UK-produced environmental documentaries reached its zenith during the years 2015 and 2016. These years marked a comparatively pivotal period when environmental concerns took center stage for the last two decades, reflected in the creation of influential titles such as "The Plastic Ocean," "Forces of Nature," and "Earth's Natural Wonders." I think such diverse selection of documentaries encompassed a wide range of topics, capturing the attention of audiences and contributing to the ongoing environmental discourse.

Throughout this project, I remained cognizant of the intricacies of Netflix's licensing process.

The distribution of environmental documentaries is influenced by myriad factors, and my analysis offers only a snapshot of this complex system. Furthermore, the dataset I used only includes data up to September 2021. An entire universe of content has been added since then, awaiting discovery.
